Engine Configuration: The suit uses a "tripod" of thrust, with one or more of the engines typically placed on the arms for steering and on the back for main lift—generating over 1,000 horsepower.

The pilot directs flight by adjusting their arm positions, which vectors the thrust.

Fuel & Flight Time: The suit runs on jet fuel or diesel, offering around 5–8 minutes of flight time.

Safety & Tech: It includes a helmet with a heads-up display showing fuel/engine data. While capable of high altitude, it is mostly used at low altitudes for safety.
